# Restaurants.ai Social + Canva Batch

Last updated: 2026-05-29

## Purpose

Use this batch to promote Restaurants.ai / RestaurantAIbot as a simple AI-assisted marketing helper for local restaurants.

## Voice

- local
- practical
- plain English
- owner-friendly
- not hype-heavy
- not tech-confusing
- focused on customers, menus, reviews, and visibility

## Core Message

Local restaurants do not need more confusion. They need clearer websites, better menus, stronger local visibility, and simple marketing habits.

## Social Post Batch

### Post 1: Main Positioning

Most restaurants do not need more complicated tools.

They need the basics done better:

- clear mobile website
- easy menu
- updated Google listing
- steady social posts
- review responses
- simple slow-night promotions

Restaurants.ai helps organize that work.

### Post 2: Owner Pain Point

A customer checks your restaurant online before they visit.

Can they quickly find:

- your menu?
- your hours?
- your location?
- your phone number?
- what makes you worth visiting?

If not, that is the first fix.

### Post 3: Menu Copy

Menu descriptions matter.

“Chicken sandwich” is okay.

“Crispy chicken sandwich with house slaw, pickles, and spicy mayo” sounds like food.

Clear menu copy helps customers decide faster.

### Post 4: Google Business Profile

Restaurant owner checklist:

Check your Google listing this week.

- hours correct?
- menu link working?
- recent photos?
- phone number right?
- reviews answered?
- holiday hours updated?

Small fixes can make the restaurant look more alive.

### Post 5: Review Responses

Restaurant reviews deserve human replies.

A good response is:

- short
- calm
- thankful
- specific
- not defensive

AI can help draft it, but the restaurant should sound like a real local business.

### Post 6: Slow Night Promo

Slow night idea:

Pick one quiet day.

Create one simple offer.

Post it the same day every week.

Make it easy to understand in one sentence.

Do not make customers solve a puzzle to save $3.

### Post 7: AI Without Hype

AI should not make a restaurant sound fake.

Used well, it can help with:

- menu descriptions
- post ideas
- review replies
- local event captions
- simple website copy

The owner stays in control.

### Post 8: Mobile First

Your restaurant website should work on a phone first.

Customers are hungry. They are moving fast.

They need:

- menu
- hours
- address
- call button
- order/call info

That is the homepage job.

### Post 9: Starter Checkup

The Restaurant Growth Starter Checkup looks at:

- website first impression
- mobile homepage
- menu clarity
- Google listing
- review response style
- social post rhythm
- slow-night ideas

Simple fixes first.

### Post 10: Honest Marketing

Good restaurant marketing does not need fake promises.

It needs:

- clear information
- real food photos
- updated hours
- easy menu
- local trust
- steady reminders

That is where Restaurants.ai starts.
