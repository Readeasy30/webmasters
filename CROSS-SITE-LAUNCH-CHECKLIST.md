# Cross-Site Launch Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before promoting any Wholelychit website publicly.

This is a planning and review checklist. It does not add live ads, payments, analytics, or third-party integrations by itself.

## 1. Repository readiness

- README.md explains the site purpose.
- AGENTS.md exists.
- AGENT-INSTRUCTIONS.md exists or repo-specific AI rules are included in README.md.
- PROJECT-STATUS.md or launch notes explain current state.
- Tech stack is clear.
- No unused starter template is visible on the live site.
- No duplicate main CSS or JavaScript files are confusing the site.

## 2. Homepage readiness

- Page title is clear.
- Meta description exists.
- Canonical URL is correct.
- Main headline explains the site in plain language.
- Main call-to-action works.
- Visitor can understand the value within 5 seconds.
- Mobile layout is readable.

## 3. Navigation readiness

- Main navigation links work.
- Back/home links work.
- Footer links work.
- Important pages are no more than 1 to 2 clicks from the homepage.
- Broken or empty links are fixed or removed.

## 4. Trust and support pages

Recommended before public promotion:

- About page
- Contact page or contact instructions
- Terms page
- Privacy page
- Advertising or affiliate disclosure page when relevant
- AI disclaimer page when AI guidance is used
- Community rules page when user/community content is planned

## 5. Search readiness

- robots.txt exists.
- sitemap.xml exists.
- Important pages have unique titles.
- Important pages have meta descriptions.
- Internal links connect related pages.
- Consumer pages use clear 7th to 9th grade language where possible.

## 6. Content readiness

- Homepage copy is clear.
- At least one next content cluster is planned.
- Articles or resource pages avoid unsupported claims.
- Helpful pages answer real user questions.
- Draft content has a clear owner, purpose, and target visitor.

## 7. Monetization readiness

Planning may exist before launch.

Do not activate monetization until trust pages and disclosures are ready.

Review these items:

- Monetization method is written down.
- Disclosure language exists when needed.
- Sponsored or affiliate content is clearly labeled when used.
- No income guarantees are made.
- No health, legal, financial, safety, or gambling promises are made.
- Payment processing, if ever added, must use a trusted third-party provider and must not store card data in the site repo.

## 8. AI feature readiness

For sites using AI guidance:

- AI limits are explained.
- AI is positioned as help, not a guaranteed expert.
- Sensitive topics tell users to use qualified professionals when appropriate.
- Pet, education, restaurant, and entertainment content avoids overpromising.

## 9. Site-specific launch notes

### ReadEasy30

- Reading app works.
- Lesson navigation works.
- Parent/helper guidance exists.
- Bubbles tone is calm and encouraging.
- Assessment/progression plan is documented.

### MathEasy30

- 30-day path is clear.
- Practice instructions are simple.
- Parent/helper guidance exists.
- Worksheet/resource plan is documented.

### PetNeeds.ai

- Pet categories are clear.
- AI guidance is limited and safe.
- Product guidance has disclosure planning.
- Local sponsor model is planning-only until approved.

### Restaurants.ai

- Restaurant owner value is clear.
- Local pages or lead-capture plan is documented.
- Claims about rankings, reviews, prices, hours, allergies, and food safety stay cautious.

### SlotsFreeUSA

- Adult entertainment positioning is clear.
- Risk language exists.
- No win guarantees or income claims are made.
- Affiliate or advertising disclosures are planned before monetized content.

## 10. Final pre-promotion check

Before posting publicly:

1. Open the homepage on desktop.
2. Open the homepage on mobile.
3. Click the main action.
4. Click navigation links.
5. Click footer links.
6. Review legal/support pages.
7. Confirm sitemap and robots exist.
8. Confirm no unfinished placeholder text is visible.
9. Confirm no sensitive setup details appear in the repo or site.
10. Commit final launch-readiness changes.

## Launch report format

After a launch-readiness pass, report:

1. Site reviewed.
2. Files changed.
3. Commits made.
4. Launch blockers.
5. Safe next queue.
