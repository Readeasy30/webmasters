# Email-Ready Contact Standards

Last updated: 2026-06-02

Use this checklist before sending any ReadEasy30 or MathEasy30 outreach email.

## Contact types

### 1. Direct verified public email

Use only when the email appears on an official organization page and is relevant to education, programs, community outreach, library reference, adult learning, parent support, or partnerships.

Status label:

`Direct verified public email`

Allowed action:

Send one polite one-to-one message. Follow up once after 7 to 10 days only if appropriate.

### 2. Contact-form only

Use when the organization provides a form but no relevant direct email.

Status label:

`Contact-form only`

Allowed action:

Submit one form message. Track submitted date and follow-up date if the form gives a reasonable route.

### 3. Directory/source only

Use when the workbook has a directory listing but no official organization contact route yet.

Status label:

`Directory/source only`

Allowed action:

Research the official website and contact page before outreach.

### 4. Hold/media-only

Use when the only public email is for press or media and the message is not a press announcement.

Status label:

`Hold/media-only`

Allowed action:

Do not email. Use the organization contact form or program route instead.

### 5. Blocked/no-contact

Use when Gmail blocks the send, the organization asks not to be contacted, the route is irrelevant, or the email is not safely verified.

Status label:

`Blocked/no-contact`

Allowed action:

Do not retry blindly. Use a safer official contact form route or stop.

## Verification checklist

Before email is marked sendable, confirm:

1. The source is an official website for the organization.
2. The email is publicly listed.
3. The email role is relevant.
4. The message is useful and not promotional spam.
5. The contact has not been sent the same message already.
6. The record includes source URL, send date, status, and next step.

## Follow-up rule

One follow-up only after 7 to 10 days.

Never run repeated follow-up chains.

## Opt-out rule

If a person or organization says stop, mark:

`Blocked/no-contact`

Do not contact again.
