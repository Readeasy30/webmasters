## Video goal

Introduce MathEasy30 as a calm math practice site for learners who need one small step, hints, and confidence before speed.

## Target viewer

- Parent helping a learner with math
- Adult rebuilding basic math confidence
- Tutor or helper
- ESL learner who needs clear math language
- Special education helper using calm support

## Length

30-40 seconds

## Style

- Vertical 9:16
- Calm math visuals
- Large readable captions
- Soft blue, teal, cream, and white
- Simple numbers and friendly cards
- Voice should be clear and slow
- No speed-test pressure
- No guaranteed-result claim

# Exact 5-Scene Canva Build

## Scene 1 — Hook

**Time:** 0-4 seconds

**On-screen text:**

Math practice should not feel like a panic test.

**Voiceover:**

Math practice should not feel like a panic test.

**Visual:**

Simple title card with calm math icons.

**Canva note:**

Use the word “panic” carefully. Keep the visual calm, not scary.

---

## Scene 2 — Core idea

**Time:** 4-11 seconds

**On-screen text:**

One small skill at a time.

**Voiceover:**

MathEasy30 helps learners slow down and practice one small math skill at a time.

**Visual:**

One simple equation, such as 2 + 1 = 3, or number blocks.

**Canva note:**

Do not make the math look hard in this video.

---

## Scene 3 — Skill path

**Time:** 11-23 seconds

**On-screen text:**

Counting
Addition
Subtraction
Word problems
Fractions

**Voiceover:**

Start with a gentle math check. Then practice counting, addition, subtraction, word problems, math facts, and fractions.

**Visual:**

Five simple cards or a gentle path graphic.

**Canva note:**

Use a path/steps layout if possible.

---

## Scene 4 — Support method

**Time:** 23-32 seconds

**On-screen text:**

Use hints.
Use drawings.
Go slowly.

**Voiceover:**

Use hints. Use drawings. Use fingers or objects if needed. Confidence comes first. Speed comes later.

**Visual:**

Bubbles-style encouragement card or pencil/dots drawing.

**Canva note:**

This should reassure parents and learners.

---

## Scene 5 — Call to Action

**Time:** 32-40 seconds

**On-screen text:**

Try MathEasy30.com free

**Voiceover:**

Try MathEasy30 free at MathEasy30.com.

**Visual:**

Site name and link with a simple button-style graphic: Start Math Check.

**Canva note:**

Hold the final link long enough to read.
