# ReadEasy30 Library and Nonprofit Outreach Kit

Last updated: 2026-05-29

## Purpose

Use this kit to share ReadEasy30 with libraries, literacy nonprofits, adult education programs, ESL groups, and tutoring nonprofits.

Goal: ask them to review and possibly share ReadEasy30 as a free reading-practice resource.

## Safe outreach rules

- Send one personal email at a time.
- Do not mass email strangers.
- Do not exaggerate claims.
- Do not claim medical, disability, or guaranteed learning results.
- Do not say official school placement.
- Ask for review, not automatic endorsement.
- Track every contact in the tracker CSV.

## What to track

For each contact, record:

- organization type
- organization name
- contact person
- role
- email or contact URL
- website
- city/area
- date found
- date contacted
- status
- follow-up date
- notes

## Status labels

Use these:

- Not Contacted
- Sent
- Replied
- Link Added
- Not a Fit
- Follow Up Later
- Bad Contact

## Next action after replies

If they reply positively:

1. Thank them.
2. Ask what type of reading worksheet would be most useful.
3. Record the request.
4. Turn repeated requests into new worksheet pages.

If they decline:

1. Thank them.
2. Mark Not a Fit.
3. Do not argue.

## Safe claim reminder

Use:

- free reading-practice resource
- calm reading practice
- printable worksheet
- adult-friendly
- ESL-friendly
- functional literacy
- parent/helper support

Avoid:

- guaranteed improvement
- certified curriculum
- special education treatment
- dyslexia treatment
- official grade placement
- school replacement
