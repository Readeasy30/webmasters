## Social Caption Batch

### Post 1: Homepage Basics

A hungry customer checks your restaurant online.

Can they quickly find:

- menu
- hours
- location
- phone number
- what makes you worth visiting

If not, that is the first fix.

CTA: Start with the Restaurant Owner Resources page.

### Post 2: Menu Copy

Menu descriptions matter.

Weak: Chicken sandwich

Better: Crispy chicken sandwich with house slaw, pickles, and spicy mayo.

Clear menu copy helps customers decide faster.

CTA: See Restaurant Menu Copy Tips.

### Post 3: Review Replies

Restaurant reviews are public customer service.

Good replies are:

- short
- calm
- thankful
- specific
- human

CTA: Use the review response templates as a starting point.

### Post 4: Slow Night

Pick one quiet day.

Create one simple offer.

Make it easy to understand in one sentence.

## Short Video Scripts

### Video 1: 3 Things Your Restaurant Homepage Must Answer

Format: vertical short video, 30 to 45 seconds

Hook:

Your restaurant homepage has one job: help hungry customers decide fast.

Beat 1:

First, say what kind of food you serve.

Beat 2:

Second, make the menu, hours, phone, and address easy to find.

Beat 3:

Third, tell customers what to do next: call, order, reserve, or visit.

Close:

Clear beats clever. Start with the Restaurant Growth Checklist.

CTA text:

RestaurantAIBot.com → Owner Resources

### Video 2: Better Menu Copy Helps Customers Decide

Format: vertical short video, 30 seconds

Hook:

Vague menu copy makes customers hesitate.

Example:

Instead of “Chicken sandwich,” say “Crispy chicken sandwich with house slaw, pickles, and spicy mayo.”

Why it works:

Customers can picture the food faster.

Close:

Short, clear descriptions usually beat fancy wording.

CTA text:

See Restaurant Menu Copy Tips.

### Video 3: Reviews Need Human Replies

Format: vertical short video, 30 seconds

Hook:

Your review replies are part of your restaurant brand.

Beat 1:

Thank the customer.

Beat 2:

Answer the point calmly.

Beat 3:

For serious issues, move the conversation to direct contact.

Close:

Do not argue online. Stay human.

CTA text:

Open Restaurant Review Response Templates.
