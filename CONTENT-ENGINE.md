# Content Engine

This is the repeatable system for creating content across Wholelychit websites without starting over each time.

## Goal

Turn one useful idea into many simple pieces of content:

1. Website article or page
2. Short video script
3. Social post
4. Canva graphic
5. Email/newsletter note
6. Internal link opportunity

## Weekly Content Flow

### Step 1: Pick One Audience Problem

Use one clear problem per content batch.

Examples:

- ReadEasy30: A learner needs calm reading practice.
- PetNeeds.ai: A pet owner needs a clear beginner checklist.
- Restaurants.ai: A restaurant owner needs more local customer attention.
- SlotsFreeUSA: A beginner wants simple free-play information.

### Step 2: Pick One Main Content Type

Choose one:

- how-to guide
- beginner checklist
- comparison article
- FAQ page
- local/service landing page
- short video script
- printable/download idea

### Step 3: Use Simple Structure

For consumer content, use plain language.

Recommended structure:

1. Clear title
2. Quick answer
3. Why it matters
4. Step-by-step help
5. Common mistakes
6. Helpful next step
7. Soft call to action

### Step 4: Repurpose

Each main piece becomes:

| Format | Output |
|---|---|
| Website | Article, guide, FAQ, or landing page |
| YouTube Shorts | 30-45 second script |
| TikTok/Reels | Same short script with hook |
| Facebook | Helpful post with simple CTA |
| LinkedIn | Business or education angle |
| Pinterest | Canva pin idea |
| Reddit | Helpful discussion prompt, no spam |
| X | Short tip thread |

## Content Quality Rules

- One clear reader problem per page.
- Use direct titles.
- Avoid fancy words when simple words work.
- Add examples.
- Add internal links.
- Make the next step obvious.
- Do not promise results or income.
- Do not present professional advice as certainty.
- Keep safety-sensitive topics general and encourage qualified help when appropriate.

## Strong Hooks

Use these formats:

- Before you try [thing], check this first.
- Most beginners miss this simple step.
- Here is the calm way to start.
- A better way to practice in 10 minutes.
- Three signs it is time to slow down and review.
- Do this before spending money.

## Soft Calls to Action

Use helpful, low-pressure CTAs:

- Start with Day 1.
- Read the beginner guide.
- Save this checklist.
- Try the free practice page.
- Compare your options.
- Come back tomorrow for the next step.

## Review Before Publishing

Before publishing, check:

- Is the title clear?
- Is the first answer fast?
- Is the page helpful without hype?
- Is there one clear next step?
- Are claims safe and realistic?
- Are links working?
- Is the content easy to read on mobile?
