## Posting Rule

Keep each post helpful by itself. Do not make people click just to get basic safety guidance.

## Social Posts

### Post 1: New Pet First Night

First night with a new pet?

Keep it simple:

- quiet space
- water nearby
- safe bed or habitat
- no loose cords or cleaners
- short family rules
- vet contact saved

A calm first night helps everyone.

### Post 2: Vet Visit Prep

Before your pet’s first vet visit, write down:

- age if known
- food brand/type
- behavior changes
- symptoms
- questions
- vaccine records if available

A short list helps the visit go better.

### Post 3: Cat Litter Box Basics

Cat litter box tip:

Place the box somewhere quiet, easy to reach, and not right next to food and water.

If a cat suddenly avoids the box, do not just assume bad behavior. Health, stress, box setup, or cleanliness may be involved.

### Post 4: Fish Tank Tip

Beginner fish tank reminder:

Clear water does not always mean safe water.

Fish need the right tank size, filtration, water testing, and steady care.

### Post 5: Dog + Child Safety

Teach children this pet rule:

Let dogs rest when they are eating, sleeping, hiding, or holding a toy.

Kind space is safer than forced hugs.

### Post 6: Pet Supplies Without Overspending

New pet supplies can get expensive fast.

Start with true basics:

- food and water
- safe bed/habitat
- cleaning supplies
- ID if needed
- safe toys
- vet contact info

Add extras later.

### Post 7: PetNeeds.ai Mission

PetNeeds.ai is for everyday pet owners who want simple answers.

What to do.
What to watch for.
When to call a veterinarian.

Helpful first. Sales later.

## Short Video Scripts

### Video 1: New Puppy Checklist

**Hook:** Bringing home a puppy? Start with these five things.

**Script:**
Food and water bowls. A safe sleeping space. Puppy food. A leash and ID tag. A vet visit plan.

Before puppy comes home, check cords, cleaners, medicine, trash, and small toys.

**CTA:** Get simple pet checklists at PetNeeds.ai.

### Video 2: Signs Your Pet Needs a Vet

**Hook:** When should you call a vet?

**Script:**
Call a veterinarian if symptoms are serious, sudden, painful, or getting worse.

Watch for trouble breathing, heavy bleeding, repeated vomiting, sudden weakness, or severe distress.

**CTA:** PetNeeds.ai gives general pet-care information, not diagnosis.

### Video 3: Goldfish Need More Than a Bowl

**Hook:** A goldfish bowl is usually not enough.

**Script:**
Goldfish need space, clean water, filtration, and steady care.

A simple aquarium set up correctly is better than a tiny bowl that looks cute but causes problems.

**CTA:** Learn beginner pet-care basics at PetNeeds.ai.

### Video 4: Turtle Found Outside

**Hook:** Found a turtle outside?

**Script:**
If it is healthy and safe, it may be best to leave it alone.

If it is crossing a road, move it in the same direction it was already going only if you can do so safely.

If it is hurt, call local wildlife help.

**CTA:** PetNeeds.ai helps with simple pet and animal safety questions.

### Video 5: Family Pet Rule

**Hook:** One family pet rule every child should learn.

**Script:**
Give pets space when they eat, sleep, hide, or seem scared.

Gentle habits protect the child and the pet.

Adults should supervise young children around pets.

**CTA:** Find simple family pet safety tips at PetNeeds.ai.
